Help on Filters & Scripts

You can use the avscript prepared for your file that would be translated to its equivalent in ffmpeg. Avscripts are actually run on avisynth platform which in detail is fed into an encoder like x264, x265 etc. Avisynth allows users to apply filters on videos upon encoding. You can darken the character lines by using appropriate filters, for example.

Translating the avscript to ffmpeg has its limitations, however. ffmpeg can apply filters but they are strictly dependant on the build of ffmpeg. I can, let's say, compile my own ffmeg binary with some filter that were not present in official ffmpeg build. Compiling your own ffmpeg build can be tricky, if you are new to it. Hence, it's advisable not to use complex filters here as they won't be parsed & translated by the parser I have written. As the time goes by and ffmpeg's coders keep on adding filters compatibility, there might be a time when you can get away by inserting complex filters as well.
Consider the following sample for the avscript that I generally use.
source = "geki12"
video = FFVideoSource(source + ".mkv")
audio = FFAudioSource(source + ".mkv")
AudioDub(video, audio)
Trim(0, 2878) ++ Trim(5036, 30809) ++ Trim(32967, 34046)
##>frame_rate=23.976
geki12 is the filename without extension.
.mkv is the extension of the file.
geki12.mkv will be fed to ffmpeg.
Trim command in avscript will invoke ffmpeg to cut file depending upon frames in avscript.
statements starting with ##> accepts custom options to feed ffmpeg.
statements starting with ##> are considered as commends in avscript.
